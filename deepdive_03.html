Deep Dive 3 (Problem Definition and Data Acquisition)

Q1:

What is the high-level description of the problem/question you want to help answer through the presentation of a collection of geographic data over the internet?

A:

Quite often the news media airs stories regarding the destruction of entire towns in the midwest. I am interested in emergency management (a plan to recover from disasters, natural or manmade). I would like to identify the Emergency Management infrastructure (fire stations, police stations, etc.) in the state of New Mexico. This map will display the distribution of infrastructure in the state.

Q2:

Who is the target audience for the information you want to provide?

A:

This map is targeted primarily at the residents of the State of New Mexico. Alternatively, the audience may also be other idividuals who are interested in emergency management.

Q3:

What geographic region does your problem area represent? Please describe it in words (e.g. New Mexico, Alberta Canada, etc.) and define it in terms of a geographic (WGS84) (latitude and longitude) bounding box.

A:

This map will be focused solely on the state of New Mexico. (EPSG:4326) LatLongBoundingBox minx="-108.758" miny="31.7845" maxx="-103.127" maxy="36.9052".

Q4:

What types of data do you want to include in your project? Include a description (data content: e.g. elevation data, hydrographic survey, etc.) and types (i.e. raster, vector).

A:

I plan on using vector data from New Mexico Resource Geographic Information System Program (RGIS). This data will display the locations of infrastructure.

Q5:

What projection will you use for the presentation of your project data? Again, describe it, provide your reasoning for selection, and provide the corresponding EPSG code.

A:

EPSG:4326. Nearly all of the data I've found was compatible with this coordinate system.

Q6:

Where do you anticipate acquiring data for the project from?

A:

New Mexico Resource Geographic Information System Program (RGIS).
